Portfolio Investment Thesis
Concentrated Quality — Differentiated from Consensus

This portfolio is built around a single organizing principle: own businesses with durable competitive advantages in sectors undergoing structural change, while deliberately avoiding the crowded Magnificent Seven consensus that dominates most institutional and retail allocations.

The top three positions — PLTR, V, and AVGO — represent three distinct expressions of the AI infrastructure buildout. Palantir captures the data intelligence layer, Broadcom the custom silicon layer, and Visa the financial infrastructure that processes the commerce enabled by the broader digital economy.

The middle six positions provide sector diversification without sacrificing quality. HII benefits from sustained defense spending with long-duration government contracts. KNSL represents a best-in-class specialty insurer with superior underwriting discipline rarely found in financial services. IBKR is the infrastructure play on capital market activity. TMO and CRM offer exposure to life sciences infrastructure and enterprise AI adoption at what Morningstar estimates as meaningful discounts to fair value.

The final two positions — O and CTAS — serve as ballast. Realty Income provides monthly dividend income and low correlation to growth. Cintas offers compounding recurring revenue with a decades-long track record of market outperformance. Combined, they reduce portfolio volatility without sacrificing long-term return potential.

Risk Considerations
Valuation Risk PLTR trades at a significant premium to intrinsic value. Sustained high multiples require continued execution. Position sized at 15% reflecting conviction balanced against valuation risk. Elevated
Concentration Top 3 positions represent 39% of the portfolio. Deliberate and thesis-driven but creates meaningful idiosyncratic exposure. Diversification across sectors partially mitigates this. Moderate
Rate Sensitivity Realty Income (O) and growth names carry duration risk in a sustained high-rate environment. The income allocation is limited to 6% to manage this exposure. Moderate
Defense Spending HII depends on U.S. federal defense budget stability. Geopolitical tailwinds are currently supportive. Long-duration contracts provide near-term revenue visibility. Contained
AI Cycle Risk Multiple holdings benefit from AI infrastructure buildout. A deceleration in enterprise AI spending would affect PLTR, AVGO, and CRM simultaneously. Partially offset by non-AI positions. Moderate

Selection Methodology
01
Business Quality Screen
Each candidate must demonstrate durable competitive advantage: pricing power, network effects, switching costs, or cost advantages. Screened for return on invested capital above 15% over 5 years. Eliminates commoditized businesses regardless of growth rate.
02
Structural Tailwind Filter
Each position must benefit from a multi-year secular trend independent of the economic cycle. AI infrastructure buildout, defense spending expansion, digital payments adoption, and specialty insurance market growth each qualify. Pure cyclical exposure is excluded.
03
Valuation Discipline
No position is sized above 12% unless the growth rate justifies the multiple on a PEG basis. PLTR's 15% sizing reflects AIP platform optionality as a non-linear upside driver. All other positions trade below 30x forward earnings except CTAS, which earns its premium through compounding consistency.
04
Position Sizing Logic
Position size is determined by composite conviction score (quality, valuation, growth, catalyst). Maximum single position is 15%. Income and defensive positions are capped at 6% each. The result is a portfolio with meaningful active share versus the S&P 500 index while maintaining controlled risk at the portfolio level.
